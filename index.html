Youth is the Future

Countless times we`ve heard the phrase “Youth is the future” or
“Children are the hope of our nation.” Society has made it clear to us that the youngsters hold the course of our future. It is engraved in all minds of teens or children
that they will one day be the one in-control of the steering wheel of our society. However, the future of our nation is now trapped in a concerning state. Each year, students face mental health problems and, worse, attempt suicide.


In the Philippines, mental health issues affect 10% to 15% of children
aged 5 to 15, and close to one in five Filipino youths aged 15-24 have considered ending their lives. During the academic year of 2021-2022, 404 young students took their own lives, while 2,147 others attempted suicide. Research suggests that factors such as bullying, marital problems, unfavorable home circumstances, school,
and peer pressure contribute to students' mental health issues.

Mental Health Matters

Mental health is an essential component of children's overall health and
well-being. It encompasses their emotional, mental, and behavioral well-being, affecting their thinking, feelings, and actions. It also plays a significant role in how they cope with stress, relate to others, and make healthy choices. Mental disorders in children manifest as significant changes in their usual behavior, emotions, and learning abilities, causing distress and difficulties in their daily routines. Common mental disorders diagnosed in childhood include attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD), anxiety, and behavior disorders.

Build a Safe Place for You and I

The children in our society desperately need our help. We should recognize the importance of their mental well-being and their emotions. It is time to break this cycle of trauma and neglect, and put an end to the agony that is slowly deteriorating them. Hence, we must build a safe place for these students, a place where they can breathe out all the toxic air they`ve been breathing in from their surroundings, without the fear of judgment from others.
